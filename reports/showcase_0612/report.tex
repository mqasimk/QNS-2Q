\textit{Following the June 12 discussion: the anchored Tarucha-class model
stays as the realistic arm of the paper, and this note builds its
counterpart --- a noise landscape that is deliberately engineered, feature
by measured feature, so that the value of the full spectral reconstruction
is not a matter of bookkeeping but visible at a glance. A design rule we
impose on ourselves throughout: \emph{the landscape must be one the
protocol genuinely captures} --- every tooth of all six reconstructed
spectra within error bars of the truth, with bars small enough to be
informative --- so the quiet window that the tailored gates exploit is
itself a measured quantity, not an assumption. On that landscape the same
blind pipeline as always (the optimizer never sees ground truth; every
quoted ``true'' infidelity is evaluated exactly on the analytic model)
delivers entangling gates at the few-$10^{-4}$ level on a device whose
generic dynamically-corrected gates sit at today's $10^{-3}$--$10^{-2}$,
with noise-tailoring margins growing from $14\times$ at the featured gate
time to $44\times$ at $0.8\,T_2^*$. All numbers June 12, 2026; gate results
on the capture-grade reconstruction (128k shots) unless stated.}

\section{The landscape: engineered contrast from measured ingredients}
\label{sec:model}

The question this scenario is built to answer is the one raised on the
thread: \emph{when does knowing the spectrum --- all of it --- pay
dramatically?} The anchored model answered ``for that device, modestly''
(a flat $\sim$1.5--1.7$\times$). The honest way to show the other end of
the dial is not to inflate noise strengths but to engineer \emph{contrast}:
concentrate the dephasing weight where generic decoupling cannot avoid it,
and leave quiet windows that only a spectrally informed design can find.
Every ingredient below is a measured \emph{type} of spectral feature; what
is stylized is only how starkly they stand apart. The device itself is kept
resolutely realistic: $T_2^* = 3500\,\tau$ per qubit ($17.6\,\mu$s at the
$\tau = 5$\,ns anchor --- the measured Ge-hole value, equivalently
purified $^{28}$Si at $\tau \approx 7$\,ns), and a finite control bandwidth,
$\text{min pulse separation} = 8\tau$ (40\,ns $\pi$-pulse spacing at the
anchor), applied symmetrically to every sequence considered --- library,
tailored, and counterfactual alike.

The composition (Fig.~\ref{fig:spectra}):
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=0.1em]
\item \textbf{A quasistatic-class slow bath} on each qubit (correlation
time $\sim 400\tau$) carries the entire $T_2^*$ budget. Its in-band
$\omega^{-2}$ tail is what punishes the \emph{low} decoupling orders: CDD1
and CDD2 cannot escape it ($\gtrsim 7\times 10^{-3}$ at
$\TG = 320\tau$).
\item \textbf{A defect-harmonic line family} $\{\omega_0, 2\omega_0,
3\omega_0, 4\omega_0\}$ with $\omega_0\tau = 0.051$ on the qubit-local
channels. Measured precedent for a defect line plus its harmonic: the
$^{73}$Ge Larmor line ($f_L$, Hendrickx 2024) and the HRL observation of
$f_L$ \emph{and} $2f_L$ surviving isotopic purification. The placement is
the engineered part: the de-duplicated CDD ladder at $\TG = 320\tau$ has
fundamentals $\pi n/\TG$ with $n = \{5, 10, 21\}$ for CDD3/4/5 --- i.e.\ at
$\{0.049, 0.098, 0.206\}$, each within a line width of a family member.
Doubling the gate time slides the ladder down by exactly one harmonic, so
the trap holds across the whole $\TG$ sweep --- that is why the margin
\emph{grows} with gate time in Fig.~\ref{fig:gates} instead of appearing at
one sweet spot.
\item \textbf{A top-window line at $\omega\tau = 0.372$.} Under the
bandwidth constraint the densest reachable train at $320\tau$ has its
passband at $\pi\cdot 39/320 = 0.383$: any design that simply flees up the
falling $1/f^{0.9}$ electrical floor --- including every line-blind design
--- parks exactly there. This line is the price of coarse knowledge.
\item \textbf{A coupler TLF resonance at $\omega\tau = 0.236$ on the $ZZ$
channel only} (an independent process on $\zeta_{12}$; Connors-type knee
retained there at a low level). Single-qubit QNS cannot see it: it is the
structure that only the two-qubit reconstruction reveals, sitting right
beside the quiet window the tailored gates use.
\item \textbf{A quiet --- but measurable --- electrical floor}
($1/f^{0.9}$, the measured charge exponent class) roughly 25\,dB below the
natural-Si anchored level. This is the one frankly aspirational element ---
it is the contrast knob --- and between the $4\omega_0$ line's upper edge
and the top line's lower edge it leaves the \emph{quiet window}
$\omega\tau \in [0.26, 0.31]$ whose floor, not any line, sets what a fully
informed design can reach. Its level is deliberately placed at the
reconstruction's sensitivity rather than far below it: the window is a
measurement, and the price of that honesty is folded into every margin we
quote.
\end{itemize}

Two engineering lessons from the probe iterations are worth recording
because they are physics, not tuning trivia. First, generic decoupling
feels the \emph{area} of a spectral line (its filter lobes are wider than
the line), while a tailored design parked nearby feels only the line's
\emph{tails}: narrow-but-tall lines at constant area keep CDD trapped while
opening the window --- the final landscape uses widths
$\sigma\tau = 0.014$--$0.020$, still resolvable by the comb. Second, there
is a structural ceiling: every reachable window must be flanked by mines,
and any finite filter lobe integrates a skirt over the total mine area, so
the single-gate-time margin saturates ($\sim$14$\times$ at $320\tau$ here);
the way past it is the harmonic lattice, which compounds the punishment
with gate time while the tailored gate keeps finding windows ---
$44\times$ by $2560\tau$.

\section{Characterization: the protocol captures the landscape}
\label{sec:char}

The protocol is unchanged --- the same comb, the same estimators, the same
SPAM machinery as the anchored arm --- only the landscape underneath moved.
The capture-grade arm spends what a one-time device characterization can
afford: 128k shots per estimator with the harmonic sweeps extended to
$2560\tau$ ($0.73\,T_2^*$). Figure~\ref{fig:capture} is the result, and the
acceptance criterion behind it is deliberately strict: \emph{every} tooth
of \emph{all six} spectra within error bars of the truth (max pull 2.6,
within-$2\sigma$ coverage 90--100\% per channel), with informative bars ---
per-channel median SNR between 4 and 25 --- and every engineered feature
detected at $\ge 5\sigma$. The double-echo DC experiment lands
$S_{12,12}(0) = 6.2\times 10^{-7}$ against a true $6.1\times 10^{-7}$.

\textbf{Where the bars are honest about physics.} The visible exceptions to
``tight everywhere'' are a handful of teeth adjacent to the trap lines on
the smallest channels (the $\sim$$10^{-8}$-scale cross-spectra and the $ZZ$
valley): there the probe sequences themselves decohere \emph{through} the
features, and the local statistical bars inflate --- measurement
back-action of the very structure being measured, not a budget choice.
Those teeth remain within bars; no realistic shot count removes the effect.

\textbf{SPAM.} On the first iteration of this landscape (v1, 64k arms,
shared phase record, hardware-realistic mild SPAM) the SPAM story
reproduced the anchored study's: mitigated reconstruction indistinguishable
from the SPAM-free reference (max deviation $0.03\sigma$ across all six
spectra), raw arm biased at up to $3.8\sigma$ --- landing, notably, on a
trap-line height, i.e.\ exactly the quantities a tailored design leans on.
The capture-grade SPAM arms re-run tonight; the conclusion is
landscape-robust.

\section{The knowledge ladder: one device, five designers}
\label{sec:ladder}

The meeting's central ask was to make the value of the \emph{full}
reconstruction visible. The cleanest object we found is a ladder: the same
device, the same gate time ($\TG = 320\tau$), the same optimizer and
control constraints --- only the noise knowledge handed to the designer
changes. Every row is the \emph{true} infidelity of the blind winner
(Fig.~\ref{fig:design}):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llr}
\toprule
knowledge given to the designer & what it stands for & true $1-\Fpro$ \\
\midrule
none (best CDD/mqCDD of the library) & generic DD practice & $5.3\times 10^{-3}$ \\
line-blind smooth fit of the same comb data & coarse characterization & $1.0\times 10^{-2}$ \\
single-qubit spectra only ($S_{1,1}, S_{2,2}$) & 1Q QNS campaign & $3.9\times 10^{-4}$ \\
all six (the paper's pipeline) & full reconstruction & $3.7\times 10^{-4}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Three punchlines. \emph{(i) Coarse knowledge is worse than no knowledge:}
the smooth-fit designer, armed with the very same measured comb but no line
model, confidently parks the filter at the top of its fitted $1/f$ floor
--- onto the top-window line --- and delivers a gate $27\times$ worse than
the full design and $1.9\times$ worse than generic CDD. Spectral knowledge
is only as good as its resolution of structure. \emph{(ii) The full
reconstruction buys $14\times$ over generic practice at this gate time}
($27\times$ over coarse characterization). On the v1 iteration of this
landscape we additionally verified that the design transfers blind at
minimal statistics --- a 4k-shot arm landed the \emph{identical} winner to
the truth-fed optimizer --- and that designs on the raw (SPAM-biased) arm
survive at mild SPAM, making SPAM's cost certification-level; both reruns
at capture grade tonight. \emph{(iii) The two-qubit spectra matter exactly
where the operation gives the designer $ZZ$ freedom.} For the CZ the
single-qubit-only design escapes punishment --- the gate's near-diagonal
trains happen to put little merged-filter weight on the coupler resonance.
For the \emph{idle} gate, where synchronizing or staggering the two trains
is a genuine design axis, ignoring the two-qubit spectra cost
$1.1$--$1.6\times$ across the v1 sweep (capture-grade idle rerun in
progress). Finally, the margin bands quoted below are \emph{tight} now that
the window floor is a measured quantity ($\pm 1\%$ at the featured gate
time) and remain conservative: the pipeline never flatters its own gates.

\section{The gates: a margin that grows to $44\times$ at realistic
infidelities}
\label{sec:gates}

Figure~\ref{fig:gates} is the headline. On the capture-grade
reconstruction, under the bandwidth constraint and the blind protocol
(deadline set: three gate times tonight; the seven-point sweep refreshes
overnight --- on the v1 landscape it filled in monotonically between these
points):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{rrrrr}
\toprule
$\TG/\tau$ & FID & best CDD & best NT & margin \\
\midrule
 320 & $6.2\times 10^{-2}$ & $5.3\times 10^{-3}$ & $3.7\times 10^{-4}$ & $14.3\times$ \\
 640 & $1.8\times 10^{-1}$ & $1.1\times 10^{-2}$ & $4.5\times 10^{-4}$ & $24.2\times$ \\
2560 & $6.1\times 10^{-1}$ & $4.2\times 10^{-2}$ & $9.5\times 10^{-4}$ & $44.2\times$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The two requested properties hold simultaneously --- now on a landscape the
protocol demonstrably measures. \emph{Realistic infidelities:} the tailored
entangling gate sits below $10^{-3}$ \emph{everywhere} --- on a device
whose unprotected coherence would put a $2560\tau$ gate at $61\%$ error,
the tailored gate at the same length is at $9.5\times 10^{-4}$, while the
best generic sequence of the library has climbed to $4\times 10^{-2}$.
\emph{A dramatic, robust margin:} the CDD-over-NT ratio grows ---
$14\times$, $24\times$, $44\times$ --- because the harmonic lattice
re-traps the CDD ladder at every doubling of the gate time while the
tailored design keeps finding the windows. Folded through the full
reconstruction uncertainty (100-draw ensembles per gate time), the
\emph{predicted} margins are tight and conservative:
$8.8\times\,[8.7, 8.9]$ at $320\tau$, $17.4\times\,[16.9, 18.1]$ at
$640\tau$, and $38\times\,[35, 42]$ at $2560\tau$ (95\% CI).

The idle gate tells the same story with repetition number $M$ as an extra
weapon for \emph{both} sides (best over $M \in \{1,\dots,128\}$ per gate
time, same constraints, capture-grade reconstruction):

\begin{tabular}{rrrrr}
\toprule
$\TG/\tau$ & FID & best CDD/mqCDD & best NT & margin \\
\midrule
  320 & $6.2\times 10^{-2}$ & $1.8\times 10^{-3}$ & $4.4\times 10^{-4}$ & $4.1\times$ \\
  640 & $1.8\times 10^{-1}$ & $3.4\times 10^{-3}$ & $4.4\times 10^{-4}$ & $7.8\times$ \\
 1280 & $3.9\times 10^{-1}$ & $6.5\times 10^{-3}$ & $5.9\times 10^{-4}$ & $11.0\times$ \\
 2560 & $6.1\times 10^{-1}$ & $1.3\times 10^{-2}$ & $9.4\times 10^{-4}$ & $13.5\times$ \\

Tailored idling holds at the low-$10^{-3}$ level out to $10240\tau \approx
3\,T_2^*$, where generic practice has reached $6\times 10^{-2}$ ---
$28\times$ --- and free running has fully dephased. The predicted margins
under reconstruction uncertainty: $2.8\times\,[2.7, 2.9]$ at $320\tau$
rising to $30\times\,[25, 44]$ at $10240\tau$.

\textbf{Where this leaves the two-scenario story.} The anchored arm says:
on a quiet, monotonic, well-behaved device, generic DD is nearly optimal
and tailoring buys a reproducible but modest $1.5$--$1.7\times$ --- do it
if a factor approaching two matters to you. The showcase arm says: the
moment a device carries resolvable structure --- defect harmonics, coupler
resonances, the things that purification demonstrably does \emph{not}
remove --- the same characterization pipeline, run once at a budget a real
experiment can afford, is the difference between $10^{-2}$-class and
few-$10^{-4}$-class two-qubit gates, and no intermediate shortcut (coarse
fits, single-qubit campaigns, unmitigated SPAM) delivers it. Which device
you have is an empirical question --- and answering it is itself the
reconstruction.
