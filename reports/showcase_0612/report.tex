\textit{Following the June 12 discussion: the anchored Tarucha-class model
stays as the realistic arm of the paper, and this note builds its
counterpart --- a noise landscape that is deliberately engineered, feature
by measured feature, so that the value of the full spectral reconstruction
is not a matter of bookkeeping but visible at a glance. On it, the same
blind pipeline as always (the optimizer never sees ground truth; every
quoted ``true'' infidelity is evaluated exactly on the analytic model)
delivers entangling gates at the $10^{-4}$ level on a device whose generic
dynamically-corrected gates sit at today's $10^{-3}$--$10^{-2}$, with
noise-tailoring margins that grow from $2\times$ at the shortest gate to
$44\times$ at $0.8\,T_2^*$ --- and a knowledge-ablation ladder showing
precisely which ingredient of the characterization pipeline buys what. All
numbers June 12, 2026; gate results on the 64k-shot reconstruction unless
stated.}

\section{The landscape: engineered contrast from measured ingredients}
\label{sec:model}

The question this scenario is built to answer is the one raised on the
thread: \emph{when does knowing the spectrum --- all of it --- pay
dramatically?} The anchored model answered ``for that device, modestly''
(a flat $\sim$1.5--1.7$\times$). The honest way to show the other end of
the dial is not to inflate noise strengths but to engineer \emph{contrast}:
concentrate the dephasing weight where generic decoupling cannot avoid it,
and leave quiet windows that only a spectrally informed design can find.
Every ingredient below is a measured \emph{type} of spectral feature; what
is stylized is only how starkly they stand apart. The device itself is kept
resolutely realistic: $T_2^* = 3500\,\tau$ per qubit ($17.6\,\mu$s at the
$\tau = 5$\,ns anchor --- the measured Ge-hole value, equivalently
purified $^{28}$Si at $\tau \approx 7$\,ns), and a finite control bandwidth,
$\text{min pulse separation} = 8\tau$ (40\,ns $\pi$-pulse spacing at the
anchor), applied symmetrically to every sequence considered --- library,
tailored, and counterfactual alike.

The composition (Fig.~\ref{fig:spectra}):
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=0.1em]
\item \textbf{A quasistatic-class slow bath} on each qubit (correlation
time $\sim 400\tau$) carries the entire $T_2^*$ budget. Its in-band
$\omega^{-2}$ tail is what punishes the \emph{low} decoupling orders: CDD1
and CDD2 cannot escape it ($\gtrsim 7\times 10^{-3}$ at
$\TG = 320\tau$).
\item \textbf{A defect-harmonic line family} $\{\omega_0, 2\omega_0,
3\omega_0, 4\omega_0\}$ with $\omega_0\tau = 0.051$ on the qubit-local
channels. Measured precedent for a defect line plus its harmonic: the
$^{73}$Ge Larmor line ($f_L$, Hendrickx 2024) and the HRL observation of
$f_L$ \emph{and} $2f_L$ surviving isotopic purification. The placement is
the engineered part: the de-duplicated CDD ladder at $\TG = 320\tau$ has
fundamentals $\pi n/\TG$ with $n = \{5, 10, 21\}$ for CDD3/4/5 --- i.e.\ at
$\{0.049, 0.098, 0.206\}$, each within a line width of a family member.
Doubling the gate time slides the ladder down by exactly one harmonic, so
the trap holds across the whole $\TG$ sweep --- that is why the margin
\emph{grows} with gate time in Fig.~\ref{fig:gates} instead of appearing at
one sweet spot.
\item \textbf{A top-window line at $\omega\tau = 0.372$.} Under the
bandwidth constraint the densest reachable train at $320\tau$ has its
passband at $\pi\cdot 39/320 = 0.383$: any design that simply flees up the
falling $1/f^{0.9}$ electrical floor --- including every line-blind design
--- parks exactly there. This line is the price of coarse knowledge.
\item \textbf{A coupler TLF resonance at $\omega\tau = 0.236$ on the $ZZ$
channel only} (an independent process on $\zeta_{12}$; Connors-type knee
retained there at a low level). Single-qubit QNS cannot see it: it is the
structure that only the two-qubit reconstruction reveals, sitting right
beside the quiet window the tailored gates use.
\item \textbf{A quiet electrical floor} ($1/f^{0.9}$, the measured charge
exponent class) roughly 30\,dB below the natural-Si anchored level. This is
the one frankly aspirational element --- it is the contrast knob --- and
between the $4\omega_0$ line's upper edge and the top line's lower edge it
leaves the \emph{quiet window} $\omega\tau \in [0.26, 0.31]$ whose floor,
not any line, sets what a fully informed design can reach.
\end{itemize}

Two engineering lessons from the probe iterations are worth recording
because they are physics, not tuning trivia. First, generic decoupling
feels the \emph{area} of a spectral line (its filter lobes are wider than
the line), while a tailored design parked nearby feels only the line's
\emph{tails}: narrow-but-tall lines at constant area keep CDD trapped while
opening the window --- the final landscape uses widths
$\sigma\tau = 0.014$--$0.020$, still resolvable by the comb. Second, there
is a structural ceiling: every reachable window must be flanked by mines,
and any finite filter lobe integrates a skirt over the total mine area, so
the single-gate-time margin saturates ($\sim$14$\times$ at $320\tau$ here);
the way past it is the harmonic lattice, which compounds the punishment
with gate time while the tailored gate keeps finding windows ---
$44\times$ by $2560\tau$.

\section{Characterization: what 4{,}000 shots already buy}
\label{sec:char}

The protocol is unchanged --- the same comb, the same estimators, the same
SPAM machinery as the anchored arm --- only the landscape underneath moved.
Two reconstruction arms were run end-to-end: a 4k-shot arm (the
shot-economy question) and a 64k-shot arm (the equal-footing reference for
all gate numbers).

\textbf{Features.} Every core line tooth (within one line width of a
center) is detected at $>3\sigma$ with pulls $\le 1.9$ in both arms; the
coupler line's teeth on $S_{12,12}$ are fully resolved at 64k (the 4k arm
leaves one shoulder tooth at the consistent-but-not-significant level ---
the one case the shot escalation was designed for). The double-echo DC
experiment lands $S_{12,12}(0) = 5.48\times 10^{-7}$ against a true
$5.43\times 10^{-7}$.

\textbf{The floor is a bound, by design.} In the quiet window the truth
sits one to two orders below the shot floor of any realistic acquisition;
the teeth there reconstruct as zeros within honest bars (all 63 gap teeth
consistent, both arms). This is not a deficiency --- it is the operating
mode of characterization on a high-contrast landscape: the reconstruction
\emph{locates the structure} and \emph{bounds the quiet}, and the bound is
what the design needs. The same property makes every prediction
conservative (Sec.~\ref{sec:ladder}).

\textbf{SPAM.} The three SPAM arms (shared phase record, hardware-realistic
mild SPAM) behave exactly as in the anchored study: the mitigated
reconstruction is indistinguishable from the SPAM-free reference (maximum
deviation $0.03\sigma$ across all six spectra), while the raw arm carries a
resolvable bias --- up to $3.8\sigma$ on the $ZZ$ channel and, notably, a
trap-line tooth mis-measured at pull 2.9: on a featured landscape, SPAM
bias falsifies \emph{line heights}, i.e.\ exactly the quantities a tailored
design leans on (Fig.~\ref{fig:spam}).

\section{The knowledge ladder: one device, five designers}
\label{sec:ladder}

The meeting's central ask was to make the value of the \emph{full}
reconstruction visible. The cleanest object we found is a ladder: the same
device, the same gate time ($\TG = 320\tau$), the same optimizer and
control constraints --- only the noise knowledge handed to the designer
changes. Every row is the \emph{true} infidelity of the blind winner
(Fig.~\ref{fig:design}a):

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llr}
\toprule
knowledge given to the designer & what it stands for & true $1-\Fpro$ \\
\midrule
none (best CDD/mqCDD of the library) & generic DD practice & $3.8\times 10^{-3}$ \\
line-blind smooth fit of the same comb data & coarse characterization & $7.1\times 10^{-3}$ \\
single-qubit spectra only ($S_{1,1}, S_{2,2}$) & 1Q QNS campaign & $3.0\times 10^{-4}$ \\
all six, SPAM-unmitigated (raw arm) & skipping SPAM treatment & $2.7\times 10^{-4}$ \\
all six, SPAM-mitigated / reference & the paper's pipeline & $2.7\times 10^{-4}$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Three punchlines. \emph{(i) Coarse knowledge is worse than no knowledge:}
the smooth-fit designer, armed with the very same measured comb but no line
model, confidently parks the filter at the top of its fitted $1/f$ floor
--- onto the top-window line --- and delivers a gate $26\times$ worse than
the full design and $1.9\times$ worse than generic CDD. Spectral knowledge
is only as good as its resolution of structure. \emph{(ii) The full
reconstruction buys $14\times$ over practice at this gate time} ($26\times$
over coarse characterization), and the design transfers blind: the same
optimizer run on the 4k-shot arm lands the \emph{identical} winner --- four
thousand shots of characterization fund a $2.7\times 10^{-4}$ entangling
gate. \emph{(iii) The two-qubit spectra matter exactly where the operation gives
the designer $ZZ$ freedom.} For the CZ the single-qubit-only and raw-SPAM
designs escape punishment --- the gate's near-diagonal trains happen to put
little merged-filter weight on the coupler resonance, and the mild raw
bias, though it visibly corrupts a window ranking, is rescued by the
optimizer's refinement; their cost here is \emph{certification}, not
design. For the \emph{idle} gate, where synchronizing or staggering the two
trains is a genuine design axis, ignoring the two-qubit spectra costs
$1.1$--$1.6\times$ in true infidelity across the whole sweep
(Fig.~\ref{fig:design}d, peaking at $\TG = 2560\tau$: $1.10\times 10^{-3}$
against the full design's $6.9\times 10^{-4}$). Finally, all arms
over-predict the tailored gate's infidelity by the same factor 1.8 (the
bounded-not-resolved window floor enters as an upper limit), so predictions
are uniformly conservative --- the pipeline never flatters its own gates.

\section{The gates: a margin that grows to $44\times$ at realistic
infidelities}
\label{sec:gates}

all under the bandwidth constraint and the blind protocol:

\toprule

\midrule

  80 & $5.1\times 10^{-3}$ & $8.7\times 10^{-4}$ & $2.8\times 10^{-4}$ & $3.1\times$ \\

1280 & $3.9\times 10^{-1}$ & $1.5\times 10^{-2}$ & $4.3\times 10^{-4}$ & $35.4\times$ \\
2560 & $6.1\times 10^{-1}$ & $3.1\times 10^{-2}$ & $6.9\times 10^{-4}$ & $44.2\times$ \\
\bottomrule

\end{center}

The two requested properties hold simultaneously. \emph{Realistic
infidelities:} the tailored entangling gate sits between $2.5\times
10^{-4}$ and $6.9\times 10^{-4}$ \emph{everywhere} --- on a device whose
unprotected coherence would put a $2560\tau$ gate at $61\%$ error, the
tailored gate at the same length is still under $10^{-3}$, while the best
generic sequence of the library has climbed to $3\times 10^{-2}$.
\emph{A dramatic, robust margin:} the CDD-over-NT ratio grows monotonically
--- $2\times$, $3\times$, $7\times$, $14\times$, $24\times$, $35\times$,
$44\times$ --- because the harmonic lattice re-traps the CDD ladder at
every doubling while the tailored design keeps finding the windows. Folded
through the full reconstruction uncertainty (200-draw ensembles per gate
time), the \emph{predicted} margins are conservative and still decisive:
$7.9\times\,[7.7, 8.1]$ at $320\tau$ and $41\times\,[34, 46]$ at
$2560\tau$ (95\% CI).

The idle gate tells the same story with repetition number $M$ as an extra
weapon for \emph{both} sides (best over $M \in \{1,\dots,128\}$ per gate
time, same constraints):

Tailored idling holds below $1.7\times 10^{-3}$ out to $10240\tau \approx
3\,T_2^*$, where generic practice has reached $4.5\times 10^{-2}$ ---
$28\times$ --- and free running has fully dephased. The predicted margins
under reconstruction uncertainty are again conservative and decisive:
$3.9\times\,[3.6, 4.0]$ at $320\tau$ rising to $25\times\,[17, 33]$ at
$10240\tau$.

\textbf{Where this leaves the two-scenario story.} The anchored arm says:
on a quiet, monotonic, well-behaved device, generic DD is nearly optimal
and tailoring buys a reproducible but modest $1.5$--$1.7\times$ --- do it
if a factor approaching two matters to you. The showcase arm says: the
moment a device carries resolvable structure --- defect harmonics, coupler
resonances, the things that purification demonstrably does \emph{not}
remove --- the same characterization pipeline, at as little as 4k shots per
estimator, is the difference between $10^{-2}$-class and $10^{-4}$-class
two-qubit gates, and no intermediate shortcut (coarse fits, single-qubit
campaigns, unmitigated SPAM) delivers it. Which device you have is an
empirical question --- and answering it is itself the reconstruction.
