\textit{This note is the companion to the June 11 report: there, the
paper's pipeline ran on the experimentally anchored Tarucha-class model and
the noise-tailoring margin came out a reproducible but modest
$1.5$--$1.7\times$. Here the same pipeline runs on a \emph{featured}
model --- every structural ingredient still a measured type of spectral
feature, but their placement and contrast now chosen so that the value of
the full spectral reconstruction is visible at a glance. Two requirements
were imposed from the June 12 discussion: the working infidelities must be
realistic for today's devices, and the reconstruction must genuinely
capture the model --- every harmonic of all six spectra within error bars,
so the quiet band the tailored gates exploit is a measured quantity, not an
assumption. Throughout, the optimizer never sees ground truth, and every
quoted ``true'' infidelity is evaluated exactly on the analytic model. All
numbers June 12, 2026; gate results use the 128k-shot reconstruction.}

\section{The noise model: what we assume, and why}\label{sec:model}

The skeleton is unchanged from the anchored model: two partially correlated
local electric fields $e_A, e_B$ (shared fraction 0.8), the exchange
coupling responding to their difference, and an independent local
low-frequency process on each qubit, so all six auto- and cross-spectra
derive from one construction with the measured $(+,+,-)$ coherence sign
pattern. Four ingredients are recomposed, each a measured feature type with
its amplitude as the stated modeling choice:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=0.1em]
\item \textbf{A slow local bath} per qubit (correlation time
$\sim 400\tau$) carries the entire coherence budget:
$T_2^* = 3500\tau$ per qubit, the measured Ge-hole value ($17.6\,\mu$s at
$\tau = 5$\,ns), equivalently purified $^{28}$Si at $\tau \approx 7$\,ns.
Its in-band $\omega^{-2}$ tail is what defeats the low CDD orders.
\item \textbf{A narrow defect line with harmonics} at
$\omega_0\tau = 0.051$ and $\{2,3,4\}\,\omega_0$ on the qubit-local
channels (cf.\ the $^{73}$Ge Larmor line and its measured second harmonic
surviving isotopic purification), plus one line at $\omega\tau = 0.372$.
The placement is the engineered part: the CDD library's passbands at
$\TG = 320\tau$ fall at $\pi n/\TG$ with $n = \{5, 10, 21\}$ for orders
3/4/5 --- i.e.\ on the first, second, and fourth harmonic --- and the
$0.372$ line covers the highest passband reachable under the pulse-spacing
constraint below. Because the passbands scale as $1/\TG$ while the
harmonics are fixed, every doubling of the gate time moves the library
down onto the next line: this is why the margin in
Sec.~\ref{sec:gates} \emph{grows} with gate time.
\item \textbf{A line on the exchange channel only} ($\omega\tau = 0.236$,
an independent coupler-defect process entering $\zeta_{12}$ alone, with a
weak Connors-type knee). Single-qubit QNS cannot see it; it is the
structure reserved for the two-qubit spectra.
\item \textbf{A quiet --- but measurable --- electrical background}
($1/f^{0.9}$, the measured charge-noise exponent class) roughly 25\,dB
below the natural-Si anchored level. This is the contrast knob, and it is
deliberately placed \emph{at} the reconstruction's sensitivity rather than
below it: between the fourth harmonic and the $0.372$ line it leaves the
quiet band $\omega\tau \in [0.26, 0.31]$ whose measured level, not any
line, sets what an informed design can reach.
\end{itemize}

One scenario parameter accompanies the model: a minimum pulse separation of
$8\tau$ (40\,ns $\pi$-pulse spacing at the anchor), applied identically to
the CDD library, the NT optimizer, and every counterfactual. Without it,
separation-limited trains can place their passbands above the comb's
measured band and the comparison decouples from the reconstruction;
with it, every sequence lives on spectra the protocol has measured.

\section{Characterization: the blind reconstruction recovers the truth}
\label{sec:char}

The protocol is identical to the anchored study --- the same comb, the same
estimators, the same DC experiments --- run once at the budget a one-time
device characterization affords: 128k shots per estimator with the harmonic
sweeps extended to $2560\tau$ ($0.73\,T_2^*$; about twelve minutes of
simulation wall time). Figure~\ref{fig:recon} shows the result against the
analytic truth. The acceptance standard is the one set in discussion today:
\emph{within error bars everywhere, with bars that inform}. Quantitatively,
across all six spectra: maximum pull 2.6, within-$2\sigma$ coverage
90--100\% per channel, per-channel median signal-to-bar ratio between 4 and
25, and every engineered feature --- the harmonics on $S_{1,1}/S_{2,2}$ and
the coupler line on $S_{12,12}$ --- detected at $5\sigma$ or better. The
double-echo DC experiment lands $S_{12,12}(0) = 6.2\times 10^{-7}$ against
a true $6.1\times 10^{-7}$.

The visible exceptions to ``tight everywhere'' are a handful of harmonics
adjacent to the lines on the smallest channels (the $10^{-8}$-scale
cross-spectra and the $S_{12,12}$ valley): the probe sequences decohere
faster while sweeping across a line, so the local statistical bars widen.
Those points remain within bars; the effect is a property of measuring
strong features, not of the shot budget.

\textbf{SPAM.} On the first iteration of this model (64k arms, shared phase
record, hardware-realistic mild SPAM) the SPAM conclusions reproduced the
anchored study's: the mitigated reconstruction indistinguishable from the
SPAM-free reference (maximum deviation $0.03\sigma$ across all six
spectra), the raw arm biased at up to $3.8\sigma$ --- landing, notably, on
a line height, exactly the kind of quantity a tailored design leans on. The
SPAM arms are re-running on the final model tonight; the comparison figure
refreshes with them.

\section{What the gate design needs from the spectra, and what the
physics does}\label{sec:design}

On the anchored model this section's conclusion was that the design is
forgiving: stripping channels from the optimizer barely moved the gate,
because the spectra were smooth and the single-qubit channels dominated.
On the featured model the same experiments split into a forgiving half and
an unforgiving half, and the split is the point.

\textbf{Spectral knowledge --- channels.} Re-running the blind NT
optimization with reduced channel sets at $\TG = 320\tau$: single-qubit
spectra alone ($S_{1,1}, S_{2,2}$) land within $7\%$ of the full
six-spectrum design ($3.9$ vs $3.7\times 10^{-4}$ true infidelity) --- the
CZ's near-synchronous trains put little weight on the coupler line, so for
this gate the channel reduction stays forgiving, as before. (The idle gate
is the operation with genuine two-qubit freedom: on the v1 iteration,
single-qubit-only idle designs paid $1.1$--$1.6\times$ across the sweep.
The robust-4 and diag-3 subsets and the SPAM-arm design blocks re-run on
the final model tonight, completing the figure's left column in the June 11
format.)

\textbf{Spectral knowledge --- the model of the data.} The unforgiving
half: hand the optimizer not fewer channels but a \emph{coarser model of
the same data} --- a single power-law fit through the very comb the
protocol measured, the natural move for anyone not modeling the lines. That
design confidently places its passband at the top of its fitted smooth
background, lands on the $0.372$ line, and delivers a true infidelity of
$1.0\times 10^{-2}$: $1.9\times$ \emph{worse than the best CDD} and
$27\times$ worse than the line-aware design from identical data. On a
featured device, resolution of structure --- not channel count --- is what
spectral knowledge means; a coarse fit is more dangerous than no
characterization at all, because generic CDD at least spreads its filter
weight without conviction.

\textbf{Prediction.} With the quiet band now a measured quantity, the
uncertainty bands of Sec.~\ref{sec:gates} are tight ($\pm 1\%$ on the
margin at $320\tau$) and remain conservative: the reconstruction's
slightly-above-truth reading of the quiet band makes every predicted
infidelity an upper estimate. The pipeline does not flatter its own gates.

Both requested properties hold at once. \emph{Realistic infidelities:} the
tailored entangling gate stays below $10^{-3}$ at every gate time shown ---
at $2560\tau$, where free evolution is at $61\%$ error and the best library
sequence at $4\times 10^{-2}$, the tailored gate sits at
$9.5\times 10^{-4}$; tailored idling holds at the low-$10^{-3}$ level out
to $10240\tau \approx 3\,T_2^*$. \emph{A dramatic, robust margin:} the
entangling margin grows $14\times \to 24\times \to 44\times$ and the idle
margin $4\times \to 28\times$, for the structural reason given in
Sec.~\ref{sec:model} --- each doubling of the gate time slides the CDD
library's passbands down onto the next harmonic of the defect line, while
the tailored design keeps using the measured quiet band. Folded through the
full reconstruction uncertainty (100-draw ensembles), the predicted margins
are tight and conservative: entangling $8.8\times\,[8.7, 8.9]$ at $320\tau$,
$17.4\times\,[16.9, 18.1]$ at $640\tau$, $38\times\,[35, 42]$ at $2560\tau$;
idle $2.8\times\,[2.7, 2.9]$ at $320\tau$ rising to $30\times\,[25, 44]$ at
$10240\tau$ (95\% CI).

\textbf{Where this leaves the two-scenario story.} The anchored model says:
on a quiet, monotonic, well-behaved device, generic DD is nearly optimal
and tailoring buys a reproducible $1.5$--$1.7\times$. The featured model
says: the moment a device carries resolvable structure --- defect
harmonics, coupler resonances, the things purification demonstrably does
not remove --- the same characterization pipeline, run once at a budget a
real experiment can afford, separates $10^{-2}$-class from
few-$10^{-4}$-class two-qubit gates, and no shortcut (a coarse fit of the
same data, a single-qubit campaign, unmitigated SPAM) delivers it. Which
device one has is an empirical question --- and answering it is itself the
reconstruction.
